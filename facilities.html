Alcove A

Alcove A - Divided into three parts, the Alcove A is located at the leftmost section facing Palma Hall. The space can be used for activities that utilize small areas such as small bazaars, small booths and stands, and small group gatherings of less than 10 people.

Alcove C

Alcove C - Divided into three parts, the Alcove C is located at the rightmost section facing Palma Hall. The space can be used for activities that utilize small areas such as small bazaars, small booths and stands, and small group gatherings of less than 10 people.

AS Parking Lot (Lower)

CSSP Parking Lot (Lower) - Located in front of the Palma Hall Building, the CSSP Parking Lot Lower portion area is also open for activities such as selling booths or small to medium stands.

AS Parking Lot (Upper)

CSSP Parking Lot (Upper) - Located in-front of the Palma Hall Building, the CSSP Parking Lot Upper portion area is the elevated lot that is also open for activities such as selling booths or small to medium stands.

PH Covered Walk

CSSP Covered Walk - The CSSP Covered walk spans from Velasquez Gate to AS 101. The narrow aisle can be used for activities that utilize small areas such as small bazaars and small booths and stands. The space is strategic because students pass by the covered walk.

Audio Visual Room (PH 207)

Audio Visual Room (PH207) - Located on the 2nd floor of Palma Hall, East wing, the AVR can be utilized for events that require the use of audio and visual equipment and can accommodate up to 60 participants.

PH 1st Floor Lobby

PH 1st Floor Lobby - Located at the first-floor main entrance of Palma Hall, the 1st Floor Lobby is known for its terrazzo granolithic design. It was made by Vicente Manansala to match the mural located at the back entrance of AS 101. Its pattern of test tubes and abstract designs incorporates the college's two merged fields: the arts and the sciences. The material used to construct this design was granite. The mural at the 1st Floor Lobby, entitled "The Arts and Sciences", was also made by National Artist, Vicente Manansala in 1960. With a length of 14 meters and a width of 2 meters, "The Arts and Sciences" is the biggest mural in the UP Diliman Campus. The lobby serves as a multi-purpose space for galleries, exhibits, events, and gatherings and can accommodate more than 170 people.

PH 2nd Floor Lobby

PH 2nd Floor Lobby - Located at the 2nd floor of Palma Hall, the 2nd floor lobby is famous for its mural made by the University of the Philippines Artists' Circle (UPAC) Fraternity in 1996. The project was headed by former UPAC head Jan Carmichael Calleja. According to Calleja, the title of the mural is "Isan Daang Taon sa Bukang-Liwayway", not "Mga Natatanging Pilipino" as stated in the marker at the second-floor lobby. The UPAC was commissioned by the College of Social Sciences and Philosophy (CSSP) through former CSSP dean Consuelo J. Paz, with the recommendation of then College of Fine Arts (CFA) dean Nestor Vinluan and CFA college secretary Reuben Defeo. The lobby serves as a multi-purpose space for galleries, exhibits, events, and gatherings and can accommodate more or less 100 people.

Multimedia Room (PH 400)

Multimedia Room (PH400) - Located on the 4th floor of Palma Hall, the MMR is similar to the AVR which has audio and visual equipment open for classroom activities and events. The space can accommodate up to 170 participants.

Video Conferencing Room (PH119)

Video Conferencing Room (PH119). Located on the first floor, east wing of Palma Hall, the VCR is reserved for CSSP constituents and activities that require the use of video conferencing equipment. The room can accommodate approximately 20 participants and has high-speed internet and state-of-the-art technology required for seamless online video conferences.

PH Steps

It serves as the pathway to the main entrance of the building. The iconic steps of the Palma Hall also serve as a multi-purpose space for huge gatherings such as ceremonies, mass organizations’ rallies, or recreational activities such as Zumba, Street dance, etc. The space can accommodate less than 100 people.
